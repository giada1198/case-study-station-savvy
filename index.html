StationSavvy

A platform that provides a centralized knowledge-base about metro stations through station profiles, including station operations, navigation, and facilities.

Design Challenge

How might we connect metro riders and administrators to share knowledge about metro stations and make them easier to manage?

Flag

Riders can flag content if they find it’s incorrect.

Report

Riders report issues they see in the station to help the administrators mitigate them.

Comment

Riders also write comments about the station, which can be marked as “useful” by other riders.

Benefits

METRO RIDERS

Trustworthy knowledge-base
Easier to seek and get help

METRO ADMINISTRATORS

Issue identification
Resource allocation

StationSavvy provides riders access to a trustworthy knowledge-base about the station. The goal is to have riders and administrators each make their individual contributions on the Station Savvy website to create a good experience. Riders contribute to the website, providing useful information and helping them to seek help easier and faster, we introduce metro administrators to ensure the trustworthiness of the information as well.

Research

  • User interviews
  • Three young professionals
  • Avid travelers
  • Regular public transit users
  • Online research about travel CSCW sites
  • Waze
  • Google Map
  • Yelp
  • TripAdvisor

The most significant insights we learn from the interviews is there is not a centralized source to figure out micro logistics or interactions about a station during a trip. Often, they have to reach out to people they know already live the the place for help or spend a lot of time doing research, try to find more credible and helpful information specifically about the stations they will go to.

For Waze, to ensure the system render the best and quickest route options for users, they have a map editor feature to encourage users to report missing/inaccurate information on the map. By giving users a stake to improve the map, users feel more control and confident with their trips.

Concepts

Concept 1

Transit system Q&A forum

Tourists submit transit-related questions in a forum-type platform. Those with local expertise contribute answers to the questions. Questions and answers are tagged and organized so they can be discovered later.

Concept 2

Local guide pairing

Tourists will be matched with a local guide when travelling to ask questions about transit. To be matched, the tourist does a short quiz about the their transit plan and special considerations during travel.

Concept 3

Station profile

Municipal transit stations have profiles that locals enter essential information about using the related transit system. Tourists can regularly validate the information on the profile, and add any other relevant information, tips, comments, and questions to help other tourists in the future.

Down Selection

  • Transit System Q&A
  • Not timely answers
  • Similar to Trip Advisor, but limited to transit
  • Local Guide
  • Would be great for tourists in a perfect world
  • Local people are not motivated or available to be “on call” for this kind of support to tourists
  • Station Profile
  • Provide a centralized knowledge-base about metro station operations, navigation, and facilities
  • Help riders figure out micro-logistics and interactions of a metro station
